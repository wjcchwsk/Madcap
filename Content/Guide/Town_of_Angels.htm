Town of Angels

Lanckorona is a picturesque village located in the Lesser Poland Voivodeship, about 30 km south of Krakow. Known for its wooden architecture, charming cafes, and idyllic atmosphere, this village, often referred to as the “Town of Angels”, attracts visitors with its serene environment and beautiful views of the Beskid Makowski mountains. In Lanckorona, you can admire 19th-century houses that create a unique and enchanting ambiance.

One of the main attractions in Lanckorona is the ruins of a 14th-century castle situated on the top of Lanckorona Hill. Built during the reign of King Casimir the Great, the castle is a significant historical landmark in the region. Besides the castle, it’s worth visiting the Regional Museum, located in one of the historic cottages near the market square. The museum showcases local folklore and exhibits related to the history of Lanckorona and its surroundings.
